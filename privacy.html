Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

SoloTracks is a music player for songs you already own. It is built to keep your music — and your data — on your device. This policy explains, in plain language, what the app does and does not do.

What we don't collect

SoloTracks does not collect personal information. We do not track what you listen to, when you listen to it, how often you listen, or where you are when you listen. We do not show ads, and we do not share data with advertisers.

Data that stays on your device

Your music library, playlists, listening history, and app preferences are stored locally on your device. None of this is transmitted to us or to any third party.

Wi-Fi Transfer

The Wi-Fi Transfer feature creates a temporary local server on your device that is only reachable from other devices on the same Wi-Fi network. Files transferred this way travel directly between your devices and never touch any server we run.

In-app purchases

SoloTracks Pro is a one-time purchase processed by Apple via the App Store. We use RevenueCat to verify your purchase status. RevenueCat receives an anonymous identifier for your device — no personal information — solely to confirm whether you have purchased Pro. RevenueCat's privacy policy is available at revenuecat.com/privacy.

Crash reports

If the app crashes, your device may send anonymized crash diagnostics to Apple, depending on your device-level settings. You can manage this under Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements. We do not run any crash reporting service of our own.

Children's privacy

SoloTracks does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a revised date. The app does not currently include a mechanism to notify you of changes; checking this page is the best way to stay informed.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Email hello@solotracks.app.
